# Starting Rackets

These are **economic starting jobs**, not combat classes. Each one gives the player a believable early bias toward one later economic worldview without spoiling tightly tuned layer-2 progression such as the meltery or remnant/death-material systems.

Design law:
- A racket should feel like a **real job**.
- A racket should imply a **fun loop** the player can get distracted by for hours.
- A racket should present a **partial truth pretending to be the whole truth**.
- A racket should be expressible mostly through **items**, not hidden perks.
- A racket should point toward **layer 3 ideologies**, not skip layer 2.

Common baseline across all rackets:
- modest general-purpose early tool
- backpack
- hook / movement utility
- enough food or utility to make the start playable

Each racket then gets:
- one **signature tool**
- one **business-enabling block or station**
- a small set of **starter inputs**
- one **support tool** reinforcing the worldview

## 1. Machinist

### Public claim
**Throughput solves everything.**

### Job fantasy
You are a practical machine worker. You believe the shortest path to prosperity is getting rotational power, primitive automation, and basic material handling online before everyone else.

### Why this is a racket
This is excellent bait for experienced modpack players. In many packs, rushing Create-style infrastructure is the correct answer. Here, it is **a** correct answer, but not **the** answer. Climate, food, logistics, place, and awkward material chains still punish tunnel vision.

### Fun loop
Build -> automate -> overcommit.

### What this racket wants the player to believe
- labor-saving compounds faster than any other investment
- once the first machine line is online, the rest of the game will follow naturally
- clever throughput beats hardship

### What reality eventually teaches
- throughput is only one layer of civilization
- machines do not erase body maintenance, weather, distance, or strange inputs
- primitive automation can make you rich in one lane while leaving you poor everywhere else

### Equipment silhouette
- signature toy: wrench
- business enabler: one strong-but-incomplete kinetic starter piece
- support: goggles, small storage help, one good generalist tool
- starter inputs: just enough to begin building, not enough to solve the line outright

### Recommended starter package shape
- `create:wrench`
- one of:
  - `create:large_water_wheel`
  - `create:millstone`
  - `create:mechanical_press`
- `create:goggles`
- a few basic storage or handling helpers
- one sturdy all-purpose early tool
- backpack
- hook

### Things to avoid
- do not hand out a complete solved production chain
- do not give enough alloy/materials to skip the feeling of bootstrapping
- do not let the kit trivialize the first Create decisions

## 2. Provisioner

### Public claim
**Bodies are the real infrastructure.**

### Job fantasy
You feed people, stabilize camps, and turn ingredients into capability. You believe most players underestimate how much power comes from food variety, buffs, kitchen infrastructure, and reliable supplies.

### Why this is a racket
This one works because the nutrition/cooking side is genuinely fun enough to trap people. A player can absolutely convince themselves they have found the real secret: if everyone is better fed, better buffed, and better supplied, everything else will follow.

### Fun loop
Cook -> provision -> dominate.

### What this racket wants the player to believe
- the body is the real bottleneck in all work
- a supplied camp outcompetes any clever technical line
- food, drink, and kitchen organization are the foundation of every serious settlement

### What reality eventually teaches
- bodies are demand, not the whole supply chain
- good provisioning does not move goods, conquer geography, or conjure missing materials
- camp abundance still depends on extraction, movement, and protection

### Equipment silhouette
- signature toy: chef or brewer identity tool
- business enabler: one kitchen station
- support: curated seeds and ingredients
- starter inputs: enough to start a menu, not enough to become self-sufficient instantly

### Recommended starter package shape
- one of:
  - `farmersdelight:skillet`
  - butcher knife / prep tool
- one of:
  - `farmersdelight:cooking_pot`
  - `brewinandchewin:keg`
  - `farmersrespite:kettle`
- small curated bundle of seeds/ingredients
- one farming/prep-support tool
- backpack
- hook

### Things to avoid
- do not dump giant seed stacks that flatten the early agricultural game
- do not make it read like a generic farmer class
- do not hand out so much food that survival tension disappears

## 3. Ritualist

### Public claim
**Proper magic is civilization.**

### Job fantasy
You are a trained practitioner of respectable, formal magic. You believe procedures, apparatus, approved reagents, and disciplined ritual are the correct way to engage with the arcane.

### Why this is a racket
This is the clean public face of magic. It is persuasive because it feels educated, safe, and modern. It lets the player believe magic is fundamentally about method and correctness, without exposing the uglier upstream substrate too early.

### Fun loop
Cast -> formalize -> deny the substrate.

### What this racket wants the player to believe
- magic is a teachable discipline of correct tools and correct procedures
- the approved systems are sufficient if practiced properly
- occult extremism is unnecessary and crude

### What reality eventually teaches
- respectable magic is real, but not self-grounding
- advanced magical economies quietly depend on translated, older, dirtier inputs
- formal magic is administration as much as power

### Equipment silhouette
- signature toy: visible reformist magical implement
- business enabler: one clean ritual/apparatus-adjacent block or utility
- support: mild safe reagents, book/manual flavor, professional trappings
- starter inputs: enough to feel initiated, not enough to expose the hidden supply chain

### Recommended starter package shape
- one visible reformist casting/ritual identity item
- one small ritual or apparatus support block
- light bundle of safe-looking reagents
- one book, manual, or professional signifier item
- backpack
- hook

### Things to avoid
- do not spoil remnant/death-material logic
- do not hand out raw occult power
- do not let the kit skip into tightly tuned layer-2 magic reveals

## 4. Surveyor

### Public claim
**Access is wealth.**

### Job fantasy
You are a professional finder of routes, sites, shorelines, structures, and opportunities. You believe most players are too static and that first access to the right place is worth more than an overbuilt base.

### Why this is a racket
This becomes compelling once it is packaged around boat mobility and expedition tools. The player can very plausibly spend all day scouting coasts, rivers, structures, islands, and biomes instead of settling down and progressing sensibly.

### Fun loop
Sail -> scout -> overextend.

### What this racket wants the player to believe
- the important thing is to find value first
- mobility and map knowledge are the true economy
- everyone else is late because they are still thinking locally

### What reality eventually teaches
- reaching is not holding
- finding is not industrializing
- routes without stable endpoints are expensive daydreams
- faraway opportunity becomes obligation once claimed

### Equipment silhouette
- signature toy: boat / modular boat path starter
- business enabler: mapmaking / wayfinding / scouting utility
- support: expedition food, storage, capture or field tools
- starter inputs: enough to make travel immediately attractive

### Recommended starter package shape
- modular boat starter or best available early boat package
- `minecraft:spyglass`
- several maps or mapmaking aids
- one expedition-friendly generalist tool
- maybe leads / field utility if it fits your pack economy
- backpack
- hook

### Things to avoid
- do not make it a combat ranger kit in disguise
- do not overstuff generic treasure-hunter junk
- do not give enough gear that danger stops mattering

## 5. Outfitter

### Public claim
**Capability begins with preparation.**

### Job fantasy
You equip people for weather, distance, and hostile conditions. You believe the real bottleneck is not invention or discovery but whether people can actually go where the work is.

### Why this is a racket
This one is strong because it turns climate and survival preparation into a business. The player can get deeply invested in enabling expeditions, hardening camps, and making bad environments economically reachable.

### Fun loop
Outfit -> enable -> become indispensable.

### What this racket wants the player to believe
- survival capability is the first and most important economic gate
- bad weather and harsh environments are the real border guards
- whoever equips the expedition controls expansion

### What reality eventually teaches
- preparation opens doors but does not fill warehouses
- environmental access still depends on supply, transport, and extraction
- the ability to survive a place is not the same as the ability to exploit it well

### Equipment silhouette
- signature toy: visible survival/climate gear piece
- business enabler: sewing or outfitting station
- support: a few climate materials and expedition supplies
- starter inputs: enough to feel specialized, not enough to trivialize survival systems

### Recommended starter package shape
- one visible climate/outdoor gear item
- one outfitting workstation such as a sewing table equivalent
- a tiny bundle of hides/fibers/materials
- bottles / cloth / small field supplies as appropriate
- one practical tool
- backpack
- hook

### Things to avoid
- do not make it secretly a combat/tank class
- do not give enough insulation to erase climate gameplay
- do not turn it into just another generic survival start

# Rejected or deferred racket ideas

These are still useful worldviews, but not good starting rackets under the current rules.

## Mortuary worker / death-native starts
Rejected as starter rackets because they would either:
- spoil the tightly tuned layer-2 remnant/death-material reveal, or
- feel too toothless if stripped enough to avoid spoilers

These are better as later factions, suppliers, or world identities.

## Meltery / foundry starts
Rejected as starter rackets because they sit too close to tightly tuned layer-2 progression and collapse too easily into the automation racket.

## Rail-only starts
Interesting, but too narrow compared with Surveyor unless rail is made much more item-legible and immediately seductive.

## Trade-only starts
Weak under current assumptions because villager trading does not naturally support the kind of believable “master variable” delusion the strongest rackets need.

# Final recommended roster

## Core five
- **Machinist**
- **Provisioner**
- **Ritualist**
- **Surveyor**
- **Outfitter**

Why this set works:
- each sounds like a real job
- each can be expressed largely through items
- none need to spoil layer 2
- each suggests a different mature economic ideology
- each can plausibly distract the player for a long time

# Short UI-ready blurbs

## Machinist
Get early mechanical momentum and start building labor-saving infrastructure before everyone else catches up.

## Provisioner
Turn food, drink, and kitchen work into serious settlement power.

## Ritualist
Begin with the tools of respectable magic and build a disciplined arcane practice.

## Surveyor
Travel early, find routes and sites fast, and turn mobility into leverage.

## Outfitter
Prepare gear for harsh conditions and make dangerous places economically reachable.
